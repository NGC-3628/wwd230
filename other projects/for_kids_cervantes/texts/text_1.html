Teen banana Skin Inventor

Growing up in Istanbul, Elif Bilgin used to enjoy walking beside the Bosphorus. However, Elif noticed that there was more and more pollution, like plastic bags, in the river. The 14-year-old decided to look for a solution. At school, Elif learned that bioplastics are more environmentally friendly than normal plastics because they are made from starch. Starch is found in foods like potatoes and bananas. However, she wanted to use an organic material that isn't eaten. Then she learned that in Thailand, over 200 tons of banana skins are thrown away every day, and that's when she got her idea! The teen inventor spent two years developing her idea. She did a lot of research online and at the library and carried out 12 experiments at school and in her kitchen. Not all the experiments were successful, but Elif didn't give up, and finally, she produced a bioplastic from banana skins. She learnt that the bananas are dipped in a special liquid. Then, bananas are placed in a glass and are heated for 30 minutes At 16, she won the Science in Action Award and the Inspired Idea Award at the Google Science Fair. Her invention could help make rivers, lakes, and oceans clean again. Since then, Elif has traveled the world telling people about her invention and has studied science in college so she can continue to make a difference in our world. After too mnay tryings she finally knew that after heating banana's peel for 30 minutes They must be dried and be pressed into a paste. Then, different liquids are added to the paste and the mixture is stirred. The mixture is cooked in an oven and it is made into plastic. Thus, she won a price and knowledge.
