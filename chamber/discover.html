A little bit of our region La Laguna

The region is called La Laguna due to its five main lakes triggered by the Nazas and Aguanaval rivers. Those lakes are currently drained though their importance for the local fauna.In the year 1000 several tribes from northern Mexico and southern USA used to live here, making this area prosperous and perfect for commerce between Aztecs and Navajos. When the Spaniards arrived in 1598 they tried to evangelize the locals with no success, even though the campaigns of friendship didn't last they settled a basecamp and a copper mine. The mine and town of Ojuela was dwellt for 3 centuries. The regions weren't important for the Viceroyalty nor for Mexico after its independence war, it just became a rest-place for merchants between Durango and Parras de la Fuente (The oldest vineyard in the Americas) and Mexico City to Santa Fe currently in NM USA

The area started to bloom when President Porfirio Diaz erected a railway from Mexico City to El Paso around the century XIX. Due to endless cotton fields in the region, many merchants from England, Germany, and China were established creating banks and chambers of commerce. The area has always been rich in silver. The Company Pe√±oles knowing this started in 1899 refining silver and gold. This made the area relevant bringing more people to live here. Unfortunately, in 1911, the Mexican Revolution took the life of 450 fellow Chinese when Pancho Villa invaded the city. Leaving a heartbreaking scar of pity and sadness between the people. Years later the area shrank in importance, however, it didn't stop building the first international airport in the north of Mexico.

La Laguna's economy lies in the automotive and ore industry, commerce, and dairy products. John Deere, Nissan, and Caterpillar, among other companies have big factories here where more than 3000 employers work each. Lala the biggest dairy product company in the country has its cattle in the region, this has raised several issues among the ambientalist who protest against soil erosion owing to overproducing of lucerne for Lala's cattle. Knowing the region is semi desertic feeding such an amount of cash cows speedingly increases water shortage. However many families get their income from this company. Currently, the city holds 1 102 374 residents.
